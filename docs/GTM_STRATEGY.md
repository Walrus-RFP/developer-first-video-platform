# Go-To-Market (GTM) Strategy  
## Decentralized Video Infrastructure Platform  

### 1. Vision  
To become the primary infrastructure layer for the next generation of video-centric applications, leveraging Walrus for storage efficiency and Sui for decentralized control and ownership.

### 2. Target Segments  

#### Phase 1: Web3 Native Apps (The Pioneers)  
- **Social Apps**: Social media platforms needing durable, censorship-resistant video hosting.  
- **Gaming Protocols**: Games requiring efficient asset delivery (trailers, clips, cutscenes) with on-chain ownership.  
- **NFT Marketplaces**: Platforms evolving into "Video NFTs" that require verifiable, immutable media assets.

#### Phase 2: Independent Content Platforms (The Scale)  
- **Education/LMS**: Online course creators wanting long-term durability and protection against platform de-platforming.  
- **Niche Streaming**: Smaller VOD services seeking simple cloud-grade ergonomics without the high egress costs of AWS/GCP.

### 3. Key Value Propositions  
- **Zero Lock-In**: Assets are stored on Walrus—independent of any single central provider.  
- **Economic Efficiency**: Lower long-term storage and egress costs compared to legacy cloud vendors.  
- **Trustless Access**: Permission control anchored to Sui Move smart contracts.  
- **Developer-First**: 1-line SDK integration makes decentralized infrastructure as easy as Mux or Agora.  

### 4. Distribution Channels  

- **Developer Advocacy**: Integrating with Sui/Walrus hackathons and workshops.  
- **Open Source Community**: Positioning as a "Modern Media Pipeline" on GitHub to attract early-stage startups.  
- **Platform Integrations**: Building plugins for popular CMS/no-code platforms (e.g., WordPress, Webflow) as an "Alternative Storage Backend."

### 5. Competitive Positioning  

| Vs. Mux/Agora | Vs. Arweave/IPFS |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Why us?** Significant cost savings and transparency vs. opaque managed fees. | **Why us?** High-performance streaming (HLS) and byte-range reads out of the box. |

### 6. Roadmap to Market  

1.  **Beta Launch (Current State)**: Fully functional "Zero-Mock" architecture with HLS, ABR, and Sui Access Control.  
2.  **Dev-Center Release**: Launching advanced metrics (Observability) and more client-side SDKs (Go, Rust).  
3.  **Global Aggregation Layer**: Moving from local aggregators to a global, decentralized edge network for faster worldwide delivery.
